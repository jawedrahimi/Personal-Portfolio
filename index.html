I am an Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences student at UC Berkeley focused on building systems that stay correct and stable under real-world constraints. My work spans low-level systems, embedded hardware, and distributed execution—areas where correctness depends not only on what a system does, but when it does it under noise, latency, and concurrency.

My preparation emphasizes implementation-level reasoning: instruction timing, cache coherence, synchronization, communication channels, and semiconductor device behavior. These experiences shaped how I approach engineering—treating timing, resource contention, and failure surfaces as fundamental design elements rather than afterthoughts.

My current research and engineering work centers on temporal correctness in distributed cyber-physical systems and embedded sensing platforms for sustainable agriculture. Across everything I build, my goal is consistent: to create systems that are deterministic, observable, and resilient.
